Job Description

Registered Nurse

Responsible for the directing of patient care for a designated shift. Reports to the Nursing Coordinator and coordinates with all related departments within limits of patient care issues for a designated shift.

Job Requirements

Minimum Education

  • Graduate of an accredited school of nursing.
  • Bachelor’s degree in nursing (BSN) preferred.

Minimum Work Experience

  • Minimum 1 year post-graduate acute care hospital experience preferred.
  • Minimum 1 year medical-surgical nursing experience preferred.
  • Experience preferred in appropriate clinical care areas.

Required Licenses

  • Registered Nurses
  • Current RN licensure in the state.
  • CPR certification.
  • Basic Life Support (BLS) certification required.
  • ACLS, PALS, or NRP certification per specialty/unit requirements.
  • Advanced certification in field of specialty / expertise preferred.

Required Skills

  • Ability to function independently, and as part of a team.
  • Empathy for the needs of patients.
  • Effective oral and written communication skills.
  • Ability to communicate through spoken and written English.
  • Detail oriented, with ability to prioritize and multitask.
  • Ability to apply sound judgment with clinical skills for optimal patient care.
  • Demonstrates flexibility by accepting and supporting changes in work assignments as related to department and patient needs.
  • Strong interpersonal communication and customer service skills.
  • Basic computer skills.
  • Proficient in working with all ages and patient populations.
  • Knowledge of appropriate equipment, policies, procedures, and strategies to promote effective patient outcomes.

Functional Demands

Physical

  • Prolonged periods of standing and walking.
  • Lifting, pushing, pulling up to 40 pounds.
  • Transferring, repositioning patients.
  • Reaching, stooping, bending, kneeling, and crouching for patient care functions.

Mental

  • Ability to perform effectively in a stressful and fast-paced environment.

Other

  • Ability to pass all required health and other screening tests.

Organization Expectations

Professionalism

  • Maintains competency and knowledge of current standards of practice, trends, and developments in related scope of practice through reading, seminars, conferences, and continuing education.
  • Participates in ongoing quality improvement activities.

Service

  • Promotes culture of safety for patients and employees through proper identification, reporting, documentation, and prevention.
  • Maintains hospital standards for a clean and quiet patient environment to maintain a positive patient care experience.
  • Demonstrates excellent customer service through his/her attitude and actions, consistent with the standards contained in the Vision, Mission, and Values of the organization.

Compliance

  • Adheres to infection-control policies and protocols, medication administration and storage procedures, and controlled substance regulations.
  • Maintains compliance with organization’s policies, as well as established practices, protocols, and procedures of the position, department, and applicable professional standards.
  • Complies with organizational and regulatory policies for handling confidential patient information.
  • Adheres to professional standards, hospital policies and procedures, federal, state, and local requirements, and TJC standards and/or standards from other accrediting bodies.

Essential Functions

Patient Care

  • Identifies patient care requirements by establishing rapport with patients and families.
  • Maintains a compassionate and caring environment by providing emotional, psychological, and spiritual support to patients, and their friends and families.
  • Provides direct patient care to assigned patients and assists in the care of other patients as needed.
  • Administers medications and nursing interventions to achieve progress toward positive patient outcomes, or to stabilize a patient’s condition and/or prevent complications.
  • Assesses patients’ response to medication, therapy, and treatments and makes appropriate adjustments and modifications to treatment plan as indicated or directed by supervisor or physician.
  • Creates a plan of care, individualized to patient needs and reflective of patient goals.

Communication

  • Assesses educational needs of patient and family regarding medication, care, therapy, and diet, and tailors communication to the individual’s needs.
  • Empowers patients and promotes positive outcomes by establishing patient care goals; teaching patient, friends, and family about condition, medications, and self-care skills; and listening to concerns and answering questions.

Teamwork

  • Supervises care, treatment, and services of patient care provided by LPNs, CNAs, and other staff on assigned unit and shift.
  • Reports patient concerns and changes in condition to supervisor and/or physician in a timely manner.
  • Maintains continuity among nursing teams by documenting and communicating actions, irregularities, and continuing needs.
  • Fosters collaboration between health care teams by communicating information, responding to requests, building rapport, and participating in team problem-solving activities.

Other

  • Maintains confidentiality and security of patients’ medical information.
